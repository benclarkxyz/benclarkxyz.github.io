Half-life of Caffeine

It is useful to understand the half-life of caffeine for several reasons. It can help you better control your tolerance, addiction, and accumulation of caffeine in the body. Caffeine is a complex drug and the human body is far more so. So, of course, it will affect everyone differently, but it seems that for most people the half-life of caffeine is about 5.7 hours.1 A small cup of black blonde roast coffee from Starbucks has 180 mg of caffeine in it.2 That means that after 24 hours there is still 5.4% of the original dose of caffeine in your body. It takes nearly 38 hours to reduce the caffeine in your body down to 1% of the original dose. So, if you drink a cup at 8 am on Monday, it will take until 2 pm on Tuesday to reduce the amount of caffeine below 1% of the original dose. Then, if you wish to keep your tolerance from increasing, it makes sense to allow time to past without a significant amount of caffeine in your system.

Additionally, caffeine's half-life means that if you drink caffeine every day at 8 am, over time the amount of caffeine will build up. Below you can change the time and amount of each caffeine dose.
